Grace Brewster Murray Hopper

Pioneer of Computer Programming

Hopper believed that computers would someday be widely used and helped to make them more user friendly. (Courtesy of the Computer History Museum)

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1906 - Born in New York City, NY
  • 1928 - Graduated Vassar with a bachelor's degree in mathematics and physics
  • 1930 - Earned master's degree at Yale University
  • 1934 - First woman to earn Pd.D in mathematics from Tale University
  • 1941 - Promoted to Associate Profressor at Yale University
  • 1943 - Joins the US Navy (WAVES).
  • 1944 - Assigned to the Bureau of Ships Computation Project at Harvard University as a lieutenant, junior grade. Co-authored three papers on the Mark I computer, also known as the Automatic Sequence Controlled Calculator
  • 1947 - Her associates discovered a moth that was stuck in a relay; the moth impeded the operation of the relay. While neither Hopper nor her crew mentioned the phrase "debugging" in their logs, the case was held as an instance of literal "debugging." For many years, the term bug had been in use in engineering.
